Pauley Pavilion

April 25-27

LA Hacks is Home.

LA Hacks is excited to celebrate its 13th anniversary as Southern California’s premier collegiate hackathon! Join us as we welcome over 1000 participants, mentors, volunteers, and sponsors to UCLA in April 2024!

Tracks
Clean Code
Clean Code
Build like the planet depends on it! This track challenges you to create solutions that empower communities and nurture ecosystems – think community gardening apps, platforms promoting shared resources, or tools that encourage eco-friendly lifestyles. It's time to write some clean code for a cleaner, more sustainable future!
Hack2School
Hack2School
Class is in session! Build projects that spark curiosity and critical thinking. From classroom management tools to AI-driven personalized learning systems, create tech solutions that empower educators and enhance student success – so let’s go hack2school!
Cold Hard Cache
Cold Hard Cache
Ideas are the most valuable currency – use them to make money move! Projects in this track enhance financial literacy, innovate investment platforms, or explore cutting-edge solutions using blockchain technology. Let’s build smarter systems to rake in the cold hard cache!
Heart of the Matter
Heart of the Matter
The doctor is in! This track invites you to tackle healthcare challenges and promote well-being from head to toe. Whether you’re building mental wellness platforms, enhancing diagnostics, or designing fitness apps that get hearts pumping, your remedies are vital for us to stay in peak condition. At the end of the day, your health is the heart of the matter!

Past Projects
  • MediBuddy
    Arnav Roy, Stanley Sha, Emma Wu, Choidorj Bayarkhuu
    MediBuddy is a website powered by an AI disease prediction model that works to solve the problem of diagnostic errors in healthcare that lead to hundreds of thousands of deaths every year. The AI model provides the physician with confident inferences about whether a patient likely has a certain condition MediBuddy acts as a virtual physician’s assistant that can help physicians reinforce their diagnoses and compile personalized reports of recommendations for patients.
    View on Devpost
  • SoundScape
    Chtun Htun, Matt H, Prateek Mishra, Prakul Singh
    Soundscape offers a unique solution that seamlessly combines location-based audio tailoring with real-time adaptive music generation. Utilizing GPS and preference data ensures a continually fresh and engaging listening experience. Users can experience a range of soundscapes, from calming nature melodies at a park to upbeat rhythms in urban settings, each crafted through a sophisticated AI-driven composition engine. Soundscape suggests new tracks for users to listen to, and can potentially incorporate social features, allowing users to discover and share popular soundscapes within their community.
    View on Devpost
  • MarkedDown
    Kush Agarwal
    MarkedDown is a web application and Chrome extension that help you watermark, identify and trace your digital media online. Since it is bundled as a SaaS platform, it is ready-to-use from signup. The process is as simple as uploading your image, downloading the watermarked copy and using that for all your media.
    View on Devpost
  • peculiar grass touching tech
    Samantha Prestrelski, Nick Petrone
    Our app utilizes your device's camera to detect if you are 1) outside on the grass and 2) touching or petting it. The longer you pet grass, the more points you get! We used React Native, a JavaScript-based UI framework for iOS and Android apps. We used TensorFlowJS for the hand pose model and Google Gemini to figure out if there was grass in the image. We also built a demo web version that is based on React.
    View on Devpost
  • FormFixer
    Rory Hemmings, My Tran, Amanda Wang, Maxine Wu
    FormFixer revolutionizes your fitness journey by offering a seamless platform for analyzing exercise form with precision. By uploading videos of your workouts, such as barbell squats, FormFixer employs advanced pose estimation algorithms and heuristic analysis to meticulously assess crucial metrics like shoulder alignment and squat depth.

What is LA Hacks?
LA Hacks is UCLA’s premier annual hackathon - uniting talented students from across the nation for an exciting, 36-hour experience of innovation and collaboration. Participants have a unique opportunity to create groundbreaking projects, connect with like-minded peers, and push the boundaries of technology and creativity in an unparalleled environment. For more details on what the hackathon is like, check out @lahacks on Instagram!
Who can participate in LA Hacks?
LA Hacks is open to college students of all skill levels, from first-time hackers to experienced coders. If you are passionate about technology, creativity, and problem-solving, LA Hacks is the perfect place for you!
Where and when does LA Hacks take place?
LA Hacks is hosted at UCLA. The specific date and venue details are announced closer to the event, so stay tuned for updates.
